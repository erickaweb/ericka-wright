Highlights

Initiatives that moved the needle

Isolated Execution Environment

In 2018 — years before congressional investigators and the FTC began scrutinizing tax prep companies for transmitting sensitive taxpayer data to Meta and other third parties via tracking pixels — I identified the risk and partnered with Security and Engineering to architect an Isolated Execution Environment for TurboTax's post-authentication web experience.

This sandboxed zone prevented third-party scripts from accessing authenticated customer sessions, ensuring that sensitive financial and tax data — protected under IRS Sec. 7216 and the FTC Act — could not be exposed to advertising pixels. While other tax prep companies were later issued FTC penalty notices with potential civil liability of up to $50,120 per violation, Intuit had already built the technical safeguard. The IEE protected the company from significant regulatory exposure and brand damage before the industry even understood the threat.

The Cookie Diet

As browser privacy capabilities — Safari's Intelligent Tracking Prevention (ITP), Firefox's Enhanced Tracking Protection (ETP), and others — began systematically degrading third-party cookie reliability, I recognized early that TurboTax's advertising measurement infrastructure was overexposed to signal loss.

I created The Cookie Diet — a strategic workstream to reduce reliance on third-party cookies by adopting emerging alternatives: cookie rewriting techniques, server-side tracking, and new privacy-preserving capabilities from key vendors including Google and Meta. The playbook established a phased migration path that ultimately led TurboTax toward Data Clean Rooms — enabling audience matching and measurement without relying on the degraded cookie signal at all.

Consent Architecture & the Business Impact Playbook

I led the onboarding of OneTrust as Intuit's Consent Management Platform, establishing the technical and governance foundation for compliant data collection across TurboTax's web and app properties. Beyond implementation, I drove the testing of a new consent paradigm — rethinking how and when consent was presented to users to maximize both compliance and addressable audience.

Critically, I created a playbook ensuring that any change to the consent UI or placement was evaluated for business impact before launch — forecasting the effect on addressable audience, media performance, and revenue. This rigor transformed consent from a legal checkbox into a strategic lever, ultimately growing TurboTax's consent rates by 3X+ and dramatically expanding the pool of users reachable through paid media.
